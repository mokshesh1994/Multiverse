The Other Side
thetrollmonk
He pushed frantically at the buttons on the enormous machine in front of him; his tired, old eyes reflecting the years of hard work that is finally reaching its culmination. As the enormous creature coughed and shivered, the lights inside the laboratory went haywire. A twenty-inch screen flickered and came to life, and his eyes met incomprehensible static. Tired of his life and of everything else around him, he had always wondered how things would turn out to be in the future, and if it would get any better, as promised by the copious printed and digital forms of fiction he had gorged on as a child. He watched with eager eyes and a pulsating heart as the image in front of him stabilized.  

*There was no grass on the other side.*
